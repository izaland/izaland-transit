Izarail · Capital Suburban Network

Sainðaul Commuter Rail

The Capital Suburban Network (首都圏廣域捷運 shutokwen-kwannwiki-shōun) is Izarail's commuter and regional rail network radiating from Sainðaul Central, one of the busiest stations in the world with around 3 million daily users. Lines extend north and south-west across the Tandan Strait and through the greater capital area, connecting the urban core with dozens of satellite cities.

The railway network expands north and south-west from Sainðaul Central Station. From here, conventional and IZX high-speed trains cross the Tandan Strait, linking the city centre with Bajikoe (West) and other destinations on Kubori Island, while other lines connect the capital to the main cities on the Ulethian side of Izaland.

The main cross-strait rail link runs through the Millennium Tunnel — a 3 km, 6-track undersea connection between Shin-Ikashuni and Kasakuri. A second cross-strait link is provided by Metro Line X, connecting Sainðaul's northern suburbs directly to central Bajikoe. A third link, the Kawashaku Line, serves Kawayatsu north of Sainðaul on the Ulethian side.

Millennium Tunnel

The primary fixed link between the two sides of Sainðaul. At 3 km with 6 tracks, it connects Shin-Ikashuni on the continental side to Kasakuri on Kubori Island, carrying both conventional commuter services and IZX high-speed trains.

The Loop Line encircles the historic heart of Sainðaul. It did not come into existence as a single project — rather, it evolved from the gradual amalgamation of various railway segments connected over decades, with the official circular service launching in 1937. Standing out on maps in its distinctive Prussian blue, the line is a daily artery for 1.7 million riders and a hub of urban activity.

Some services terminate at Hayatogaru Station, which hosts the line's maintenance depot. During rush hours, headways compress to 90 seconds to accommodate peak demand on one of the busiest commuter loops in Uletha.
